Background on Covid19

COVID-19 is a respiratory disease that primarily impacts the lungs and airways. The virus belongs to a family of coronaviruses, which affects both animals and humans. This virus emerged and was discovered in December of 2019 in the Chinese city of Wuhan and quickly spread globally, leading to a pandemic. The Covid-19 virus was confirmed to be in Jamaica by The University Hospital of the West Indies (UHWI) on 10 March 2020. This first case of the virus in Jamaica was a young female that travelled from London to Jamaica on March 4, who had developed symptoms compatible to COVID-19 on March 7 and visited the Accident and Emergency Division of the UHWI on March 9, 2020. COVID-19 symptoms typically appear 2-14 days after virus exposure, with the majority of individuals experiencing minor symptoms. However, older persons and people with prior health problems are more likely to develop serious complications such as pneumonia and acute respiratory distress syndrome.

COVID-19 symptoms may first resemble flu symptoms, such as a scratchy throat, a runny nose, and a lack of fever. As a result, many individuals do not take the infection as seriously until it is too late. As the virus multiplies and develops, symptoms such as fever or chills, coughing, shortness of breath/difficulty breathing, weariness, muscle or body pains, and headaches will appear. COVID-19 is typically transmitted through the mouth or nose of an infected individual in the form of minute liquid particles when they cough, sneeze, speak, sing, or breathe (droplet transmission). It can also be transmitted when infectious particles in the air are inhaled at close range (short-range airborne transmission)  or by touching contaminated surfaces.

A vaccine developed by BioNTech and Pfizer was licensed for emergency use in the United States on December 2, 2020. It was first marketed for the prevention of COVID-19 disease for individuals sixteen (16) years of age and older. However, the vaccination is now accessible for children aged twelve (12) to fifteen (15), as well as for a third dosage in some immunodeficient persons. As of 2:09pm CEST on October 12, 2023, there had been 771,191,203 confirmed cases of COVID-19 recorded to the World Health Organization, including 6,961,014 deaths. A total of around thirteen million vaccine doses had been delivered as of October 5, 2023.

Tips To Protect Yourself From Covid-19

Follow the THREE Ws:

1. WASH your hands

Wash your hands frequently for at least 20 seconds with soap and water, especially after being in a public area or after coughing, blowing your nose, or sneezing. Ensure to carry and use a hand sanitizer containing at least 60% alcohol if soap and water are unavailable.

2. WEAR a mask

Go to the nearest hospital or health clinic if you have a cough, fever or have difficulty breathing. Frequently clean and disinfect touched objects especially devices. Refrain from using any device in the bathroom.

3. WATCH your distance

Keep a minimal of six feet between you and others. Use a bent elbow or tissue to cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.

4. Take your accines

COVID-19 vaccines assists your body to fight the virus that causes COVID-19. However, those who are vaccinated may still get infected with the virus which is why staying up to date on COVID-19 vaccines will significantly lower the risk of getting infected.

5. Get tested for Covid-19

A If you have COVID-19 symptoms, you should immediately get tested for COVID-19. If you have been exposed to COVID-19 and do not have symptoms, you should still get tested five full days after your exposure. If not tested at the right time, an inaccurate test result is likely.

6. Avoid contact with people who have been exposed to or been confirmed with Covid-19

By avoiding contact, the risk of catching the virusis reduced. If staying away from a person who has COVID-19 is not practical, use as many prevention strategies as you can like wearing masks and washing your hands regularly.
